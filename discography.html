Discography

Aurora's music blends elements of art pop, dream pop, alternative folk, and electropop. Her sound is characterized by atmospheric synths, ethereal vocals, and poetic, introspective lyrics. Deeply inspired by nature and Nordic traditions, her songs often explore themes of emotion, spirituality, and the human condition.

All My Demons Greeting Me as a Friend

Aurora's debut album blends dream pop, electronic, and folk in a deeply emotional journey. It explores inner struggles, innocence, and empowerment, introducing her haunting vocals and poetic vision to the world.

View all

Infections of a Different Kind (Step 1)

A more experimental and political release, this EP-album hybrid explores themes of connection, activism, and identity. Aurora's sound becomes more layered and bold, with tracks like Queendom and Forgotten Love celebrating empowerment and vulnerability.

View all

A Different Kind of Human (Step 2)

The continuation of Step 1, this album delves into human consciousness, environmental concerns, and spiritual awakening. It features rich production, tribal rhythms, and cinematic intensity, especially in songs like The Seed and The River.

View all

The Gods We Can Touch

Inspired by Greek mythology, this album examines morality, divinity, and human desire. It combines baroque pop, synth-pop, and art pop in an elegant, theatrical sound. Highlights include Cure for Me, Heathens, and Giving In to the Love.

View all

What Happened to the Heart?

Aurora's latest album is a meditation on reconnection—with ourselves, others, and the planet. With a softer, organic tone, it blends acoustic elements, subtle electronics, and intimate vocals. It's her most introspective and healing work to date.
